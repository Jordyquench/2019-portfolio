Isometric Illustrations

A New Style

I decided to start a side project and seen some designs using the isometric style and thought I would give it a go.

While I was having a look through Pinterest and Dribbble for some inspiration I seen an IOS game called "Monument Valley". The style for this game was perfect for what I thought would be a good place to start, obviously a way simplier version.

The first Illustration wasn't suppose to be what it turned out to be. I just started on a blank hex grid trying to understand how to create things in this style and it turned into something that looked kind of like the game.

The first Illustration

Starting from scratch

I explained before briefly about how I started this design, but quickly it started out with no plan because this wasn't meant to be the design. I was just putting shapes together and seeing what I could create.

The more I added the more it started to look like a scene, and the more I was learning. I kept wanting to add new things and maybe about half way through I started to use more complex things like curves, then after that I moved onto some optical illusions and it all worked out really well.

I'ts All Coming Together

A showcase of the final product

After seeing everything coming together was quite exciting, I was really happy with the way things were looking, so the next step was to bring all the screens into invision and make it look like a real working app.

This was my first time using invision, I was instantly surprised by how simple the interface was and how easy it was to create a working prototype. In terms of the prototype, it works so well in showing the user how to navigate the app.
